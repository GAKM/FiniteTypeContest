About the experiment

In this experiment, you and other participants will be competing in a contest to win monetary prizes by submitting costly bids. 
Each participant's final payout will be
 

Endowment - Bidding cost + Prize won.
 

The endowment, bidding costs, and prizes will be denominated in tokens, and you will be paid 1 US dollar for every 50 tokens in your final payout. So if your final payout is 225 tokens, you will be paid 4.5 US dollars.

We will now describe each of these three ingredients that make up the final payout.

1) Endowment 

Each participant's endowment is {{ C.ENDOWMENT }} tokens. 

2) Bidding cost

Each participant will be assigned a cost-per-bid, which will be either {{ C.COST_LOW }} token or  {{ C.COST_HIGH }} tokens, with equal probability. 

Participants will not be informed about the cost-per-bids assigned to other participants.

If a participant is assigned a cost-per-bid of {{C.COST_LOW}} token, and he bids {{ C.SAMPLE_BID }}, the bidding cost will be {{ C.SAMPLE_BIDDING_COST_LOW }} tokens. 

And if a participant is assigned a cost-per-bid of {{ C.COST_HIGH }} tokens, and he bids {{ C.SAMPLE_BID }}, the bidding cost will be {{ C.SAMPLE_BIDDING_COST_HIGH}} tokens.

3) Prize won
Each participant's prize won (in tokens) will be determined by his bid and the bid(s) of his opponent(s) in the contest. The exact details about how bids determine prizes will be described later.

Participants will now be required to complete a comprehension task to be able to participate in the experiment.
